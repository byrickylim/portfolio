Corporate Event Production

Technical Director

End-to-end technical direction for a corporate general session — translating client goals into a designed audio, video, and lighting system, routing every source through a redundant signal path, managing the crew, and executing a clean, on-time show for stakeholders and a live audience.

01

Client Goals

The client needed a corporate general session that looked and sounded professional, reinforced their brand, and ran exactly to a tight agenda. Executive presenters, presentation content, and a live audience all had to be supported without a single visible technical stumble.

  • Clear, intelligible audio for presenters and playback throughout the room.
  • Polished video — branded content, presentation slides, and image magnification (IMAG) so the back of the room sees the stage.
  • A clean, on-brand stage look with reliable lighting.
  • A precise run-of-show with zero overruns and no dead air.

System Design

I designed the production as a single integrated system with redundancy at the critical points. Audio, video, and lighting were planned together on paper first — signal flow, stage plot, power, and equipment schedule — so the build matched the plan and could be troubleshot under pressure.

03

Audio Routing

All audio was routed over a Dante network into a digital console, giving a software-defined patch that was fast to build and easy to reconfigure as the agenda changed. Mics, playback, and program feeds each had a defined path to the room and to the video/record feeds.

  • Inputs: Presenter wireless mics, lectern mics, and content playback consolidated to the console.
  • Transport: Dante over managed switches with clock leadership and a redundant path defined.
  • Outputs: Discrete feeds to PA, video switcher, and any record/stream destination.
  • Documentation: Full patch sheet and input list so the system was reproducible and supportable.
04

Video Systems

The video package combined presentation content, branded graphics, and live IMAG through a single switcher feeding the main screens, so the audience always saw the right source cleanly and on cue.

  • IMAG: Camera-to-screen magnification so presenters read clearly from anywhere in the room.
  • Content: Slides and branded playback cued to the run-of-show.
  • Switching: Live switching with multiview and confidence monitoring at the operator position.
  • Distribution: Signal converted and distributed to screens and any record/stream feed.

05

Crew Management

As Technical Director I led the technical crew across departments — assigning roles, setting the schedule, and keeping everyone on comms and on the same run-of-show.

  • Defined roles for audio, video, lighting, and stage positions.
  • Ran the load-in schedule and held checkpoints so the room was ready before doors.
  • Briefed the crew on the run-of-show and the chain of command for live decisions.
  • Maintained clear comms discipline so cues landed cleanly during the program.
06

Show Execution

During the session I directed the show — monitoring all systems live, cueing the team, and absorbing the inevitable last-minute changes without letting them reach the audience.

  • Called or coordinated cues across audio, video, and lighting in real time.
  • Monitored signal and system health continuously, ready to switch to backup paths.
  • Managed agenda changes and presenter adjustments on the fly.
  • Kept the program on time from first cue to close.
07

Results

A polished, on-time general session delivered end to end — designed, built, and run as one reliable system, with documentation that makes the next show faster.
