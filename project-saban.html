Venue Overview

The Saban Theatre is a historic single-balcony theatre in the Los Angeles area, presenting concerts, comedy, award programming, faith services, and private productions. A venue of this age and scale demands a technical approach that respects the architecture while delivering modern, broadcast-adjacent production values.

As Technical Director, I am responsible for the house technical package and for translating each incoming production's rider into a working show — bridging the gap between what visiting acts expect and what the building provides, and making both meet reliably every night.

03

Audio Systems

Front-of-house is mixed on a Yamaha CL5, fed by a Dante network that carries stage inputs from digital stage boxes into the console and out to processing and amplification. Building the audio system around Dante means the patch is software-defined, redundant, and fast to reconfigure between a comedy night, a full band, and a spoken-word service.

  • Console & mixing: Yamaha CL5 at FOH, with scene-based recall for fast changeovers between acts and event types.
  • Networked transport: Dante audio over managed switches, with primary/secondary paths and defined clock leadership.
  • Input management: Maintained patch sheets and input lists so visiting engineers can walk into a documented, predictable system.
  • Wireless & RF: Coordinated wireless mic and IEM frequencies to stay clean in a dense Los Angeles RF environment.

04

Lighting Systems

The lighting package combines house conventional fixtures with LED units controlled over DMX. For presented shows I maintain a working plot and channel hookup so visiting LDs can focus on programming rather than discovery, and so house events can be lit quickly and consistently.

  • Control: DMX-based control with documented universes and addressing.
  • Plot & hookup: Maintained lighting plot, channel hookup, and focus notes for repertory use.
  • Fixtures: Conventional and LED wash/spot fixtures for both theatrical and event looks.
  • Repeatability: Saved looks and cue stacks for recurring event formats.

05

Video Systems

Video support ranges from playback and projected content to image magnification for larger presented events. Signal is distributed and converted as needed to feed projection and house displays, with content cued to match the run-of-show.

  • Playback: Cued video and content playback synchronized to the show.
  • Projection & display: Projected content and house displays for presented programming.
  • Distribution: Signal conversion and distribution to feed multiple destinations cleanly.
06

Production Responsibilities

  • Advancing incoming productions — reviewing technical riders and reconciling them with the house package.
  • Owning load-in, soundcheck, show, and strike scheduling for the technical departments.
  • Maintaining the house patch, signal flow, and system documentation.
  • Operating front-of-house and coordinating monitors, lighting, and video as each show requires.
  • Acting as the technical point of contact for visiting engineers, tour managers, and house staff.
07

Challenges Solved

  • Historic building, modern riders: Bridged the gap between a legacy venue and current touring expectations by standardizing the house system and documentation.
  • Fast changeovers: Used scene recall and pre-built patch to turn the room around quickly between very different event types.
  • Network reliability: Designed the Dante network with redundancy and VLAN separation so audio transport stays clean under load.
  • Dense RF environment: Coordinated wireless frequencies to keep mics and IEMs stable in a crowded Los Angeles spectrum.
08

Leadership Responsibilities

Technical direction at a venue is as much about people as signal. I set the plan, brief the crew, and keep the floor calm and coordinated from the first push case to the last.

  • Leading and assigning house and supplemental crew across load-in, show, and strike.
  • Running clear comms and a defined chain of command during live shows.
  • Setting safety expectations and keeping the work inside OSHA-aligned practice.
  • Mentoring crew on systems and documentation so the venue runs consistently regardless of who is on shift.
09

Outcomes

A documented, repeatable house system that lets visiting productions load in, mix, and strike with confidence — and lets the venue say yes to a wider range of bookings.
